_Last updated: 2026-06-17 · Maintained by Inflexa, Inc._

## Why this document exists

The **Inflexa source code is open source** and you are free to use, copy, modify, and redistribute it under the terms of the project's license (see [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE)).

The **Inflexa name and logo are different**. They are trademarks of Inflexa, Inc. ("Inflexa"), and they are **not** licensed under the open-source license that covers the code. Trademarks exist so that when someone sees the word "Inflexa" or the Inflexa logo, they can trust they know where the software came from. Open-sourcing the code does not give away the brand.

This policy explains how you may and may not use the Inflexa marks. Most ordinary, honest uses are fine and require no permission. The goal is to let the community talk about, build on, and extend Inflexa freely, while preventing uses that would confuse people about what is official and what is not.

## Marks covered by this policy

- The word mark **"Inflexa"** (whether registered or unregistered)
- The **Inflexa logo** and any associated design marks
- Confusingly similar variations of the above

We refer to these collectively as the "Inflexa Marks."

## What you may do without asking us

You do not need permission for honest, factual ("nominative") references to the project. For example, you may:

- State truthfully that your tool, library, workflow, or service **works with**, is **built on**, is **compatible with**, or **uses** Inflexa.
- Write tutorials, blog posts, papers, documentation, and conference talks **about** Inflexa, and use the name to refer to it.
- Use the name in academic citations and in `CITATION.cff`-style references (see the repository's citation metadata).
- Run unmodified copies of Inflexa internally or commercially. Using the software is always free under the license; this policy only governs the name and logo.
- Link to the official project repository and website.
- Use the logo to link to or refer to the official project, provided you do not alter it and do not imply endorsement.

When you reference Inflexa, please use it as an adjective followed by a generic noun ("the Inflexa CLI," "an Inflexa workflow"), keep the capitalization ("Inflexa," not "inflexa" or "INFLEXA" outside of code/command contexts), and do not use it as the most prominent element of your own branding.

## What requires renaming or permission

To protect the community from confusion, the following uses are **not** permitted without our prior written permission:

1. **Distributing a modified version under the Inflexa name.** The license lets you fork and modify the code freely, but if you distribute a modified build, you must give it a **different name** and remove the Inflexa Marks (including the logo and any "Inflexa" branding in the CLI name, package name, and user-facing strings). This is the standard practice for open-source projects: the code is yours to change, but a changed product should not be presented to users as "Inflexa." You may, of course, state factually that your fork is "based on Inflexa" or "a fork of Inflexa."

2. **Naming your product, company, project, domain, social account, or organization** in a way that includes "Inflexa" or a confusingly similar term, or that implies your offering is official, sponsored, endorsed by, or affiliated with Inflexa, Inc.

3. **Offering a hosted, managed, or commercial service under the Inflexa name.** Inflexa, Inc. provides a commercial hosted platform (see [`COMMERCIAL.md`](./COMMERCIAL.md)). To avoid confusing users about which service is official, you may not market a competing or third-party hosted service using the Inflexa Marks.

4. **Modifying the logo**, combining it with other logos or marks, or using it in any way that suggests endorsement or partnership.

5. **Merchandise** (stickers, shirts, etc.) bearing the Inflexa Marks for sale or wide distribution.

6. **Registering** the Inflexa Marks, confusingly similar marks, or related domain names anywhere in the world.

If you'd like to do any of the above, just ask — see [Requesting permission](#requesting-permission). We're generally happy to work with community members, educators, and partners in good faith.

- Maintain clear space around the logo and do not crop it.

This trademark policy is about preventing brand confusion between official and unofficial offerings. It does not and cannot restrict your rights to the **code** under the open-source license.
